SOLENE BY ALEXG
Version 1.0

Thank you for downloading Solene.

Solene is a creative .cube LUT designed to sit on top of a clean base grade. It is not a technical camera transform, so convert log footage first or start with normalized Rec.709 footage before applying the look.

WHAT IS INCLUDED

- Solène by alexg.cube
- LICENSE.txt

QUICK START

1. Import or browse to Solène by alexg.cube in your editing app.
2. Apply it after exposure, contrast, and white balance are in a good place.
3. Dial the intensity down if needed. A good starting range is 30% to 60%.
4. Make small shot-by-shot adjustments after the LUT so the look feels intentional.

INSTALL GUIDE

Adobe Premiere Pro

1. Open the Lumetri Color panel.
2. Go to Creative.
3. Open the Look dropdown.
4. Choose Browse.
5. Select Solène by alexg.cube.
6. Adjust Intensity to taste.

DaVinci Resolve

1. Open Project Settings.
2. Go to Color Management.
3. Click Open LUT Folder.
4. Copy Solène by alexg.cube into that LUT folder.
5. Back in Resolve, click Update Lists.
6. Apply the LUT from the LUT browser or a node.
7. Adjust Key Output Gain or node opacity to control strength.

Final Cut Pro

1. Add the Custom LUT effect to your clip or adjustment layer.
2. Open the LUT dropdown.
3. Choose Custom LUT.
4. Select Solène by alexg.cube.
5. Adjust Mix to taste.

GENERAL WORKFLOW

1. Balance exposure and white balance first.
2. Convert log footage to Rec.709 before applying Solene.
3. Apply Solene.
4. Lower the LUT intensity when needed.
5. Finish with small contrast, saturation, and skin tone adjustments.

RECOMMENDED USE

- Use on normalized Rec.709 footage or after your camera-to-Rec.709 conversion.
- Put the LUT after primary correction, not before.
- Lower opacity, mix, or key output gain when the image feels too strong.
- Recheck skin tones, highlights, and blacks after applying.

SUPPORT

If the LUT does not load, make sure your app supports .cube LUT files and that you are selecting the file inside the LUTs folder.
